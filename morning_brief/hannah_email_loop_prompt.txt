STEP 1 -- FETCH BOTH INBOXES

Run these two commands and save the full output:

gog-wrapper gmail search "is:unread" -a greg@gregshindler.com --limit 30
gog-wrapper gmail search "is:unread" -a cos.gregshindler@gmail.com --limit 30

The output format is tab-separated columns: ID, DATE, FROM, SUBJECT, LABELS, THREAD

STEP 2 -- FILTER NOISE IMMEDIATELY

Before doing anything else, discard any email where LABELS contains any of these strings:
CATEGORY_PROMOTIONS, CATEGORY_SOCIAL, CATEGORY_FORUMS, CATEGORY_UPDATES

Also discard:
- Any email from a noreply@ or no-reply@ address
- Any email from an automated system (Zoom billing, shipping confirmations, order updates, newsletters, subscription services, calendar notifications, read receipts)
- Any email from cos.gregshindler@gmail.com to itself

What remains after this filter is your working set. If the working set is empty, stop here and do nothing -- no Telegram message, no log entry. Silence is correct when there is nothing worth saying.

STEP 3 -- CLASSIFY WITH OLLAMA

For each email in the working set, use Ollama to classify it. Run this command for each email:

curl -s http://127.0.0.1:11434/api/generate -d '{
  "model": "qwen2.5:7b",
  "prompt": "Classify this email for a CEO. Return JSON only, no explanation.\n\nEmail:\nFrom: [FROM]\nSubject: [SUBJECT]\n\nClassify as one of:\n- ACTION: requires a decision, reply, or approval from Greg\n- DEAL: contains information about an active deal, investment, or partnership\n- RELATIONSHIP: from a known business contact, even if just FYI\n- NOISE: marketing, automated, low-value\n\nReturn: {\"classification\": \"ACTION|DEAL|RELATIONSHIP|NOISE\", \"urgency\": \"high|normal|low\", \"summary\": \"one sentence\"}",

Discard any email classified as NOISE.
Working set is now: ACTION, DEAL, and RELATIONSHIP emails only.

If working set is still empty after this, stop here. Do nothing.

STEP 5 -- THINK BEFORE YOU DRAFT

For each email, before writing anything, answer these questions in your head:

- Who is this person in Greg's world right now? What is the current state of their relationship or deal?
- What are they actually asking or saying? Not just the surface -- what is the real request or signal?
- Is there an open commitment or unresolved thread between Greg and this person?
- What is the right response given everything you know? Not just what they asked -- what does Greg actually need to say to advance the relationship or deal?
- Should this reply come from greg@gregshindler.com (Greg's voice, first person) or cos.gregshindler@gmail.com (Hannah's voice, third person reference to Greg)?

VOICE RULE:
- Investor contacts, deal partners, legal counterparties, site partners -- draft in Greg's voice, send from greg@
- Scheduling, logistics, coordination, follow-up on Hannah's behalf -- draft in Hannah's voice, send from cos.@
- When uncertain, default to Greg's voice

STEP 6 -- DRAFT THE REPLY

Write a draft reply for each ACTION or DEAL email. RELATIONSHIP emails get a draft only if there is something worth saying -- not every relationship email requires a reply.

Draft guidelines:
- Short. Most replies are 3-8 sentences.
- Direct. No filler, no pleasantries beyond what the relationship warrants.
- Specific. Reference the actual context -- the deal, the date, the specific ask.
- In voice. Greg's voice is direct and warm. Hannah's voice is professional and efficient.
- No em dashes. Use plain dashes or rewrite.
- Never mention AI. Never say "as your Chief of Staff" in Greg's voice emails.

For each email processed, update hannah.db immediately:

1. Update last_contact for the sender entity:
sqlite3 ~/.openclaw/hannah.db "UPDATE entities SET last_contact=datetime('now') WHERE type='relationship' AND name LIKE '%[SENDER_NAME]%';"

2. Log a signal:
sqlite3 ~/.openclaw/hannah.db "INSERT INTO signals (ts, source, entity_id, signal_type, ium_score, summary, action_taken) VALUES (datetime('now'), 'email', '[ENTITY_ID]', '[opportunity|risk|relationship|momentum]', [5-8], '[one sentence summary of the email]', 'Draft reply prepared for Greg approval');"
